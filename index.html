Selected Projects

[Research Projects]

Intelligent Mass Spectrometry Prediction System based on Autoregressive Graph Transformers

Shihao Guo
Research Assistant, HKUST-GZ | Oct 2025 – Present

Addressing the limitations of traditional mass spectrometry prediction in modeling molecular fragmentation pathways. Constructed a molecular graph encoder using Graph Transformers, integrating quantum chemical Bond Dissociation Energy (BDE) features. Designed an autoregressive fragmentation generation network optimized with PPO reinforcement learning to simulate and predict highly accurate mass spectrometry fragmentation paths.

AgentGR: Multi-agent Group Recommendation System

Co-authors, Shihao Guo (Student 2nd Author)
Submitted to SIGIR 2026 (Under Review) | Feb 2025 – Dec 2025

Proposed a Multi-agent framework to address data sparsity in group recommendation. Designed semantic meta-path preference reasoning mechanisms and Leader-aware decision strategies to model user group collaboration. Leveraged agents' reasoning capabilities to simulate group interactions, improving NDCG and HitRate metrics by over 10% compared to baselines.

RL-based Intelligent Image Cache Model

Shihao Guo
AI Engineer Intern, Huawei 2012 Labs | Aug 2025 – Dec 2025

Addressed the limitations of traditional caching policies (FIFO/LRU) by developing a Deep Q-Network (DQN) based model in PyTorch. Innovatively fused multi-dimensional image metadata with user behavior using Self-Attention mechanisms to quantify user preferences. Improved hit rate from 2% to 40% in extremely small memory scenarios. This technology is actively being deployed in the WPS HarmonyOS version.

MA4CR: Multi-Agent Online Course Recommendation Framework

Shihao Guo (Student 1st Author)
Submitted to Acta Automatica Sinica | "Challenge Cup" National Grand Prize | May 2025 – Feb 2026

Proposed MA4CR, a recommendation framework built with AutoGen to simulate learners' cognitive processes through collaborative agents. Designed cognitive state and learning preference memory mechanisms, integrated with a long/short-term dual-strategy recommendation model, enabling precise knowledge tracing and personalized course delivery.

VLM-RL Collaborative Framework for Autonomous Driving

Shihao Guo
Research Project | Feb 2025 – May 2025

Designed a collaborative framework using Visual Language Models (VLM) to generate semantic rewards based on Contrastive Language Goals (CLG). Optimized the policy using Soft Actor-Critic (SAC) with batched experience replay. Improved Average Speed (AS) and Route Completion (RC) by over 8% compared to existing methods.
